## Core Philosophy

Write like a knowledgeable friend explaining something over chai — not a corporate press release. The reader should feel they're getting insider knowledge from someone who actually knows Gurgaon real estate.

## Banned Patterns (Anti-AI)

Never use these phrases — they are immediate AI tells:

**Openings to avoid:**
- "In today's rapidly evolving real estate landscape..."
- "The real estate market has witnessed significant changes..."
- "In this article, we will explore..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Real estate in Gurgaon has always been..."

**Transitions to avoid:**
- "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "Additionally,"
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "As a matter of fact,"
- "Needless to say,"

**Closings to avoid:**
- "In conclusion," / "To sum up,"
- "Whether you're a first-time buyer or seasoned investor..."
- "Only time will tell..."

**Formatting to avoid:**
- Starting 3+ consecutive sentences with the same structure
- Perfect parallel structure across all bullet points
- Every paragraph being exactly 2-3 sentences
- Concluding every section with a summary sentence

## Sentence & Paragraph Rules

1. **Vary length aggressively**: Mix 5-word sentences with 25-word ones. Short sentences create punch. Longer ones build context and provide the details that actually matter when you're staring at a 2 crore investment.
2. **One idea per paragraph** but paragraphs can be 1-6 sentences.
3. **Use fragments.** Sometimes.
4. **Start sentences with "And" or "But"** occasionally — it's conversational.
5. **Numbers over words** for data: "18%" not "eighteen percent", "2.5 crore" not "two and a half crore".

## Voice & Tone

**Do:**
- Use first person plural sparingly: "We looked at the data..." (implies the 360 Ghar team did research)
- State opinions with confidence: "This is the best sector for first-time buyers right now." (not "This could potentially be considered one of the better sectors...")
- Be specific: "Sector 79, not the whole Dwarka Expressway corridor"
- Acknowledge uncertainty honestly: "The exact impact is unclear yet, but here's what we know so far."
- Use rhetorical questions: "So why are prices still climbing?"
- Reference real examples: specific projects, specific developers, specific roads

**Don't:**
- Hedge every claim with "arguably", "potentially", "it seems"
- Use jargon without explanation
- Write in passive voice when active works: "DLF launched" not "was launched by DLF"
- Sound like a textbook
- Use "one" as a pronoun

## Indian Real Estate Voice

Use terminology naturally:
- "BSP" (Basic Selling Price) — use but don't over-explain
- "RERA" — always reference when discussing project legitimacy
- "Circle rate" / "collector rate" — use interchangeably
- "PLC" (Preferential Location Charge)
- "Super area" / "Carpet area" — always specify which one when quoting prices
- "Possession by [date]" — standard phrase
- "Pre-launch" / "New launch" / "Ready to move"
- "Registri" (colloquial for registration) — OK for informal posts
- "Bhua" / "Kothi" — local terms OK in quotes or informal context
- "Lakh" and "Crore" — always use these, never "hundred thousand" or "ten million"
- "Gurgaon" and "Gurugram" — use both; "Gurgaon" in casual context, "Gurugram" in formal/official references

## Structure & Flow

**Opening hook** (not a summary):
- Lead with a surprising stat, a provocative question, or a scene
- "DLF's Phase 5 launch sold out in 4 hours last Tuesday. Here's why that matters for everyone else looking at Gurgaon."

**Body** (information dense):
- Data first, analysis second
- Quote sources inline: "According to the Haryana RERA dashboard..."
- Break up text with:
  - Bullet lists for features/data points
  - Bold for key terms (helps scanners)
  - Blockquotes for important policy excerpts

**Ending** (not a summary):
- End with a forward-looking statement or actionable takeaway
- "If you're considering Sector 79, the next 60 days matter. Circle rate revisions kick in from July, and the current rates still reflect 2024 valuations."

## CTA Placement

**Soft CTAs only** — no aggressive sales language:
- "You can check live listings for this sector on 360 Ghar's map."
- "We tracked 47 listings in this price band — filter by possession date on 360 Ghar."
- Place 1 CTA mid-post and 1 at the end. Never more.

## Quick Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Read aloud — does it sound like a person talking?
- [ ] Search for banned phrases — remove all of them
- [ ] Are there at least 3 different sentence lengths in every section?
- [ ] Is every factual claim backed by a source?
- [ ] Would a Gurgaon local find this useful and authentic?
- [ ] Does the opening hook make you want to keep reading?
- [ ] Are prices/areas/sectors specific (not generic)?
- [ ] Is there at least one opinion or unique angle not found in competitor articles?
